After medical school, I didn't know what I wanted to do. My loving parents had encouraged me to get my doctorate, but I couldn't find any jobs I really wanted to apply for. I took a few internships throughout California, and didn't stay at any of them for very long. I had reached a dark point in my life. About 7 internships later, I came across the Murderous Flounderesses, an inverted colon research facility, and I fell in love. I was looking forward to waking up in the morning and making the long commute to work. Work had never seemed so important, or fun. It was like everything I was meant to do was put into a single job. The environment I was surrounded by, and the relationships I made was a dream come true. Our CEO, Keegan, helped me find a house nearer to work to help, shorten my long driving hours and I am forever in his debt. He's a prime example of the work community that has been established here at the Murderous Flounderesses. The work we do here is so impactful, I feel the effects everyday. I am a firm supporter of this cause, and you should be too.
